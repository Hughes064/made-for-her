CLOTHING REVIEWS

Gabba Jersey

My love affair with the Castelli Gabba Jersey wasn’t easy at first. Oh, I was in love at first site, but I often abandoned the jersey in my cart; its $200.00 price tag turning me away. Alas, I can only resist temptation so much, and after one too many morning rides shivering, I rushed home, opened my laptop, and completed the checkout process. Merely two days later (thank you, two-day shipping, Backcountry Gear!), the beautiful jersey was in my hands.

Pros

The Gabba Jersey is beautiful. It fits snugly against my torso without extra material that bogs me down; the dropped tail protects my backside from spray; and the minimalist design is aesthetically pleasing.

I am overly impressed by the warmth of this jersey. As someone who typically runs cold - I have been known to shiver when the indoor thermometer reads 72 degrees - I find myself unzipping the side vents while wearing the Gabba, even in sub 40 degrees and with only the Gabba on. The side vents let just enough coolness in so I don’t overheat, but I can still stay warm.

It’s best in its class for the wind blocking technology relative to the breathability and weight of the jersey. (Side note: Castelli worked with Gore to develop the Windstopper X technology, and this licensing is exclusive to Castelli).

Room For Improvement

My recommendations for improvement are slight, but nonetheless I think these implementations would enhance the jersey:

  • More reflective strips. Right now, there is one reflective strip on the back, and I would like to see more reflective strips on the back and the arms.

  • Offer the jersey in the highly visible yellow fluo that's available for men.

  • Have the back pockets just slightly lower for easier access.

  • The sleeves are just a tad bit too short for me, although that may be due to my slight build and average height.

Overall, and I cannot say this with enough emphasis: The Castelli Gabba jersey is a winter cycling essential. The motto for this jersey should be: “Winter riding just got better.”
